1. The **Immensity**  <br> of God's Love [(v11-12)](# "ref")

>>>
+ Doesn't **deal** w/us in anger, acc. to our **sins**

  + and His **interaction** with us:

<!-- .slide: <%= bg("unsplash-EpbIXGCrtK0-baby_feet.jpg") %> -->
The Lord is **merciful** and **gracious**,

slow to anger and  <br>
abounding in steadfast love.

[Psalm 103:8 (ESV)](# "ref")
[Janko Ferlič, Koper, Slovenia](https://unsplash.com/photos/EpbIXGCrtK0 "caption")

>>>
+ **Combo** compassionate + gracious: 11x in OT
+ Compassion ("merciful"): deep **feeling** of love
  + Often from superior to **inferior**
  + Rooted in natural **bond**, e.g., to **babies**
+ Grace: gifts, love in **action**
  + Also **superior** to inferior
  + By unearned **choice**
+ How God **intro** self to Moses on Mt. Sinai (Ex34)
  + Descended in **cloud**, passed before Moses:

---
The Lord, the Lord, a God **merciful** and **gracious**, <br>
slow to **anger**, and abounding in <br>
**steadfast love** and faithfulness

[Exodus 34:6 (ESV)](# "ref")

>>>
+ Same: **compassion** + **grace**
+ Slow to **anger** ("long nostrils"): 
  + Forcible exhalation, hot **breath** of wrath
+ Not that God **never** gets angry, but
  + not **eager** or **fickle** in punishment
+ **Just** in His anger, **desires** to show mercy:
+ Steadfast **love** (chesed): covenant lovingkindness
+ Again, in Ex33 as quoted in **Rom9**:

---
<!-- .slide: <%= bg("earth.jpg") %> -->
“I will have **mercy** on whom I have mercy,<br>
and I will have **compassion**<br>
on whom I have compassion.”

[16](# "ref")
So then it depends not on<br>
human **will** or **exertion**,<br>
but on **God**, who has mercy.

[Romans 9:15-16 (quoting Exodus 33:19) (ESV)](# "ref")

>>>
+ Same: **compassion** + **grace**
+ Essential **ingredient**: God's sovereign **choice**
+ Compassion upon small **infant** or orphaned **puppy**
  + Not bc they've **done** anything
  + But precisely bc of their **helplessness**
+ Not out of contractual **oblig.** or expect. of **reward**
  + But **freely** given, out of **charity** / humanity
+ So is **God's** compassion toward us:
+ Not what we **deserve** / entitled
  + bc what we **deserve** is His **anger**:

<!-- .slide: <%= bg("unsplash-pHR_S0PeOF0-hands.jpg") %> -->
He will not always **chide**, <br>
nor will he **keep** his anger forever.

[10](# "ref") 
He does not **deal** with us  <br>
according to **our sins**, <br>
nor **repay** us according to **our iniquities**.

[Psalm 103:9-10 (ESV)](# "ref")
[Milada Vigerova](https://unsplash.com/photos/pHR_S0PeOF0 "caption")

>>>
+ "chide": **strive**, contend, lawsuit/battle
  + "keep" ("anger" only implied): **preserve**, maintain
+ This is how God **deals** w/us (make, do, accomplish)
+ He has every **right** to **pay** us back for our sins:
  + **settle accounts**, make full, ripe/wean
+ But in His **compassion**, He chooses **not** to
+ Not that God **ignores** / blind to sin
  + "don't **worry** about evils of world"
  + "everything will be **ok**"
+ No, God is a **holy** God: 
  + sin is a **serious** issue to God
+ e.g., systematic **racism** in policing
  + vilification of **poor** + immigrant; **abortion**
  + also **my** own sin + self-righteousness

---
>>>
+ This is why Christ **died** for us:
  + didn't **hand-wave** away the problem of evil
  + but **paid** for it w/own life
+ The reality is God is **not** fair to us
  + He **chooses** to show us grace
+ Will I choose **grace** toward others?
+ "But they don't **deserve** my respect!"
  + No, they **don't** --
+ Nor did I deserve Christ's **sacrifice**:
  + Grace is **unfair**
